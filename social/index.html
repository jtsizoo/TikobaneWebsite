Community Development

Communities in Matebelend North with highest number of National parks in Zimbabwe (Hwange National park being the largest) are among the poorest in society and they also must bear the costs of living with wildlife with little to no benefit. Our community development programs are focused on improving livelihoods of these communities whose role in conservation is crucial/vital.

We are currently focused on these projects: Animal Sanctuary, Community Gardens, Old Peoples Home cleaning, career guidance, food security and environmental education programs among others

Gender Equality

Our communities tend to have gender streaming where more attention is given to gender depending on the level of education and cultural background. Thus, we have boys and girls clubs in the communities who are being educated in various topics of benefit. These clubs seem to be beneficial as they boost confidence and self-esteem of kids. Also, we are looking forward to introducing youth, women and men clubs which will equip them to make some commodities in their respective homes. Already the boys and girls club are active (we have a syllabus and weekly activities while others will be implemented in due time). Within our gender equality programs we are focused on the following: Girls Club Boys Club.

Education, Training, and Capacity Building

We are zealous about educating, training, conveying skills and building the capacity of local upcoming conservationists and other conservationists. The victory of conservation tomorrow depends on the type of active conservation leaders we groom today.

Our Training and Capacity building projects are focused on: internships, sports, debates, quiz, and public speaking, schools, conservation education, conservation camps sanctuaries, and national park visits.

Environment and Recycling

Recycling has been one of the methods we are using keep our environment. Recently we have been picking up containers using them for our Chilli concoction project. The litter in our environment tend to be a threat to wildlife since most of the litter is non-biodegradable, thus we have a project of picking litter around Dete Community. We have a legacy restoration project which targets to planting trees that are rarely seen or that are few due to elephant damages such as pod mahogany.
