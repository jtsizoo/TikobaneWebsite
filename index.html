About Us

Tikobane Trust is a registered non-governmental organization that operates in communities along Hwange National Park. Our main focuses include conservation, animal welfare, school competitions, environment and recycling, and entrepreneurship.

Our mission is to build the capacity of local communities to protect and cohabit with wildlife , while improving community livelihoods. We aim to take a scenary level approach to conservation that considers the needs of people, wildlife, livestock, pets, and the environment holistcally. We achieve this through comprehensive-scientific research, conservation action, animal welfare action, capacity building, community development and engagement, human-wildlife conflict mitigation, environmental education, and supportable living approaches.

We also concentrate on empowering communities living adjacent to Hwange National Park and improving their livelihoods. We believe that seeking and developing holistcally empowerment opportunities will have an active effect in reducing the community's dependence on illegal activities such as bush meat poaching, animal cruelty and illegal wildlife trade. We are advocating for wildlife, animal welfare, and the environment.
